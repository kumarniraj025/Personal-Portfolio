Web Design

Web design is the art and science of creating visually appealing, functional, and user-friendly websites. It combines design principles, coding skills, and user experience (UX) strategies to deliver seamless online experiences. Key aspects include layout design, color schemes, typography, and responsive behavior across devices. Web designers use tools like HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, ensuring websites are not only aesthetically pleasing but also optimized for performance and accessibility.

Web Development

Web development is typically divided into three areas: front-end development, which handles the visual and interactive aspects of a website; back-end development, which manages server-side operations, databases, and application logic; and full-stack development, which combines both. Developers use programming languages and frameworks like HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Python, PHP, and React to bring designs to life and ensure smooth functionality.

Graphic Design

Graphic design is the art of visually communicating ideas and messages through creative and strategic use of typography, imagery, color, and layout. It plays a vital role in branding, advertising, publishing, and digital media, helping to convey information effectively and make a lasting impression. Graphic designers use tools like Adobe Photoshop, Illustrator, and Canva to create designs for logos, brochures, websites, social media, packaging, and more.
